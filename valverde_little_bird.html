Match Report  ·  Real Madrid vs Manchester City
The Little
Bird flies
Valverde scores a 22-minute hat-trick, plays auxiliary right-back, and produces one of the great individual performances this stadium has ever staged
Real Madrid 3 – 0 Manchester City
Valverde ⚽ ⚽ ⚽

He walked out carrying a pennant, touched palms with the kids lining the tunnel, and stepped into the light. Forty-five minutes later he walked back inside, paused briefly, and clenched his fist. Low-key, all things considered — given what he had just done was barely believable, even by the standards of a stadium that has hosted a great many barely believable things.

On the tunnel wall there is a slogan from Alfredo Di Stéfano: no player is as good as all the players put together. That is true, of course. But it had been tested here — because Valverde sometimes seems to be all the players put together. A man with four lungs and three first-half goals, all of them brilliant.

1′ – 20′
Right-back
Madrid appeared overrun. Valverde dropped to protect, holding the structure together while the team found its footing.
20′ – 42′
The 7, 8, 9 & 10
Three goals in 22 minutes. Everywhere across the attacking third — wide, central, in the box. One man, every position at once.
45′ – 90′
Right-back again
Returned to defend. One last tackle. Clean sheet secured. Victory — his, above all.

The first goal set the tone. Courtois played an exceptional long diagonal to the right touchline where, with one touch and on the bounce, Valverde controlled and guided it past O'Reilly — who should have taken him down. Three touches, 100 yards, and Madrid had a lead that would prove unassailable. The second was taken in stride with his right and struck hard and low with his left. The third — Brahim's scooped ball, a nudge over Guéhi, a volley past Donnarumma — had a hint of Bergkamp to it, and might not even have been the best of the three.

"I've run out of words. He's the most underrated footballer on the planet — and has been for years."
— Trent Alexander-Arnold

This hasn't been an easy season for Valverde. He expressed displeasure at playing right-back, was whistled by his own fans, met with white hankies in the stands, and stood accused during Madrid's collapse. The No 8 shirt he wore tonight was Kroos's last gift to him. The weight of it was real.

He stood accused before. Tonight he stood taller than anyone. After the third goal, Bellingham's mouth fell open in a big O of disbelief — and then he just started laughing. In a different box, Mbappé clambered over a seat roaring. Valverde blew out his cheeks. Bloody hell. This wasn't supposed to happen. Only in this stadium, what's not supposed to happen is exactly what's supposed to happen.

Arbeloa's verdict
"A 21st-century Juanito." The late, loved striker was Madrid's patron saint of the impossible. "This shows that we are Real Madrid and you should never give us up for dead."
A birthday coincidence
Waiting at the tunnel was Pirri — the club's honorary president, turning 81 — the only other midfielder to score a European Cup hat-trick for this club. He had company now.
